One Piece (Anime)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

One Piece
Genre Adventure, Fantasy
Based on One Piece by Eiichiro Oda

Original run October 20, 1999 – present
Episodes 1100+ (ongoing)

One Piece is a Japanese anime television series produced by Toei Animation that premiered on Fuji TV in October 1999. It is based on Eiichiro Oda's manga series of the same name. The story follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy, a boy whose body gained the properties of rubber after unintentionally eating a Devil Fruit.

Overview

One Piece follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his pirate crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, as they explore the Grand Line in search of the world's ultimate treasure known as "One Piece" in order to become the next Pirate King.

Plot

The series begins with the execution of Gol D. Roger, the King of the Pirates, who reveals that his treasure, the One Piece, is hidden somewhere in the Grand Line. Twenty-two years later, Monkey D. Luffy sets off on a journey from the East Blue Sea to find the One Piece and become the next Pirate King.

Production

The anime adaptation of One Piece was announced in summer 1997 during a party celebrating the manga's 100th chapter. Toei Animation's team was led by producer Junjirō Tanaka and director Kōnosuke Uda. The series has been running continuously since 1999 with over 1100 episodes as of 2023.

Reception

One Piece has received widespread critical acclaim and commercial success. It holds the Guinness World Record for "the most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author." The anime has consistently ranked in the top anime series in Japan and has gained a massive international following.
